Front Matter
======
My name is Alec MacMillen, and I am an active-duty Airman in the United States Air Force. I currently serve as a Protocol Specialist at Kirtland Air Force Base in Albuquerque, New Mexico. I also hold an MS in Computational Analysis and Public Policy from the University of Chicago, and I am pursuing an MS in Computer Science from Georgia Tech. In my spare time I am a triathlete, fiction author, and FAA-certified private pilot.

I created this webpage to host my musings and data analytics projects. My primary research interests include aviation (especially with respect to automation, human factors and performance) and politics. My projects employ a variety of methods ranging from machine learning (with a focus on deep learning, convex optimization, and applied linear algebra) to more traditional discrete mathematics and algorithms.

I learn primarily by doing, and the goal of this website is to encourage myself to pursue projects I'm interested in. If any projects hosted here are of any interest to readers who may stumble across my page, I consider this undertaking to be a success.

Source Code
======
The code underlying projects presented here is hosted on my [personal github profile](https://github.com/alecmacmillen). My ethos is to remain a perpetual student, and my blind spots are numerous and wide. I always welcome the opportunity to learn from folks more knowledgeable than I.

Future Projects
------
My philosophy is to never let the perfect be the enemy of the good - I look for the most amount of learning for the least amount of extraneous energy expended. As such, many of the projects I host here will be rough, unfinished, and exploratory. Some projects I plan to tackle in the near future include:

- Bespoke implementations of machine learning algorithms including decision trees, bagging, adaboost, xgboost, support vector machines, and more. I'm interested in coding my own rough versions of these algorithms from scratch and comparing their performance to out-of-the-box implementations from sklearn on a series of toy examples, with special attention paid to classes of machine learning problems that are particularly well suited to each type of model.
- In particular, I'm interested in examining applied use cases of boosting like [this one](https://static.us.edusercontent.com/files/NVX8B4W8z9Tcrc4bEaO8uzEB) and [this one](https://static.us.edusercontent.com/files/ULVQ0auQwSn37atQTv0fMjHl).
- Ground-up implementation of A* (along with an explanation of theory) applied to a practical example near to my heart, early pre-deregulation airline route maps from the 1960s and 1970s.
- A game-playing robot that uses the minimax algorithm to beat a human player at Connect 4 (I may or may not be searching for new strategies to beat my human friend Tammy).
- As I recognize NumPy as a useful but somewhat impenetrable tool, I'm excited to dive more deeply into [tutorials](https://numpy.org/numpy-tutorials) and [applications](https://numpy.org/learn) that help [demystify](https://numpy.org/devdocs/user/quickstart.html) the C-underpinning that makes it such an efficient machine for matrix operations.
- As a resident of Albuquerque, New Mexico who is currently searching for a new apartment, I'm also interested in building a regression tree that can predict apartment prices based on characteristics like square footage,location, and amenities.
- The 2026 midterms are approaching, and mid-cycle redistricting is a hot-button issue in states ranging from California to Texas to Virginia. Geospatial data is a particular weakness of mine and I'm intrigued by the opportunity to dive into Geopandas and build interactive redistricting tools that show what-if analyses for hypothetical district boundaries in different states.
